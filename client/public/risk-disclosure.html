2. Futures Trading Risks

Futures trading carries specific risks that you should understand before engaging in such activities:

  • Leverage Risk: Futures contracts are highly leveraged instruments. A relatively small market movement can have a proportionately larger impact on the funds you have deposited. This can work against you as well as for you.
  • Market Volatility: Futures markets can be extremely volatile. Prices can change rapidly and unpredictably, potentially resulting in significant losses.
  • Margin Calls: You may be required to deposit additional funds on short notice to maintain your position. If you fail to meet a margin call, your position may be liquidated at a loss.
  • Liquidity Risk: Under certain market conditions, you may find it difficult or impossible to liquidate a position at a reasonable price.
  • Gap Risk: Markets can gap significantly between trading sessions, potentially resulting in losses that exceed your stop-loss orders.
  • System Risk: Electronic trading systems are subject to failures, delays, and errors that could result in missed trades or incorrect executions.
